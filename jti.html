JTI | PRIDE

Brief
Develop a digital campaign for a global corporation that celebrated diversity and inclusion through PRIDE

Role
Graphic Designer

Deliverables
Exploratory research, creative brainstorming, icon design, infographics, typography layout, social media content creation.

Design tools
Adobe InDesign, Illustrator, Photoshop and After Effects

As one of 3x graphic designers on this project, I supported the research of the potential challenges of cultural/D&I-led comms for employees who would likely consider themselves “straight”.

For example, the importance of Senior Leadership in pushing the agenda. The importance of allies, as they will make up a majority of your movement. The local and cultural differences we would encounter, especially at a company like JTI. The potential for discrimination, and how to combat this.

By setting out a clear roadmap for success in 2020, with a full calendar of content and activation, I was part of the team to design content that displayed creativity and innovation through both print and digital platforms.

A mix of educational, inspirational, celebratory and policy-led content meant that we could bring Pride to life in a way that would be recognised by our audience, whether they were part of the LGBT+ community or not. movement.

This project was winner for The Drum Award, B2B 2020, and has been shortlisted for a Digital Impact Award 2020, and Corporate Content Awards 2021.
